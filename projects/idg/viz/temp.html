Temperature


First, we'll look at how things change with temperature. If we raise the temperature of the gas heating container, we'll see an increase in pressure; the particles are going faster now, so they're hitting the side of the container more often. Every time one hits a side, it makes a 'pop' sound. Use the slider to see what happens to pressure when you adjust the temperature.



As you can see, pressure increases linearly with temperature; as you raise temperature, pressure increases at a similar rate.
